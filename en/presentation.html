Opening up administrations to digital talent to solve public challenges in 10 months

In 2016, the Presidency of the French Republic announced the creation of the Public Interest Entrepreneurs program, based on the observation that administrations want to carry out ambitious digital transformation projects but do not have the advanced skills required in-house for this purpose.

The program's principle: allow exceptional digital profiles to join administration for 10 months in administrations to meet the challenges of improving public services using digital technology and data.

For this purpose, each year, the program establishes a public interest entrepreneurs promotion. Data scientists, designers and developers join administrations in multidisciplinary teams of 2 or 3 to take up challenges in various fields: access to justice, integration of refugees, fight against fraud, etc.

How is a public interest entrepreneurs promotion established?

1. A promotion is established on the basis of challenges. They are selected through a call for projects in accordance with criteria of impact on the professions of administrations and citizens, technical innovation and openness to new working methods. Pour en savoir plus, rendez-vous sur la page "défis".

2. As an essential component of a promotion, public interest entrepreneurs are then selected through a call for applications. Mentors for each challenge participate in the selection panel to make sure they find their gems.To discover the recruited profiles, visit to the "community" page.

3. The promotion can then begin, punctuated by coaching sessions, peer-to-peer learning sessions and diverse events. To learn more about the coaching of promotions, go to the "coaching provision" page.

4. The program proceeds on the basis of feedback. This learning posture allows the adjusting of the coaching provision and the promotion the success of challenges. To find out more about the program's iterations, visit the "blog" page".

The program is backed by Etalab. It is funded by the Programme d'investissements d'avenir (Future investment program), led by the Secrétariat général pour l'investissement and Caisse des dépôts et consignations.
