Accompanying the State's digital transformation: the EIG method

The remit of the program coordination team: create and back a promotion of digital professionals and public servants to complete some fifteen challenges in as many different administrations.

For this purpose, the team designs a tailor-made coaching program. This is intended for both EIGs and mentors and is designed to assist them at each stage in the challenge: scoping of the challenge, definition of the roadmap, putting into production, enhancing the tools produced, sustainability of the challenge...

Coaching takes various forms:

Induction week or "EIG bootcamp"

A few weeks after the arrival of EIGs in their administrations, the induction week allows them to get to know each other, share their initial steps in the administration and discover the coaching program.

Discovering an EIG bootcamp

Plenary sessions with EIGs

One day per month, the promotion is held in a neutral location to explore a particular topic (legal framework, design, communication, etc.). Formats vary: stand-ups, presentations, workshops, peer-to-peer learning, etc.

Discovering an EIG session

Plenary sessions with mentors

For half a day a month, mentors meet in a neutral location to share their successes, their obstacles and their ideas for putting the challenges they back on a permanent footing.

Discovering a mentor session

Seminars "outside the walls" with EIGs

Discovering an outside the walls session

Coaching sessions with EIGs and their mentors

They allow teams to take stock of the progress of their challenges and to agree on their roadmaps.

Discovering an EIG and mentors session

Mediation between EIGs and their mentors

This ensures that aims are understood and shared by everyone. It also allows us to step back from the challenge and steer it in promising directions.

Networking

The team relies on synergies between EIGs and/or mentors and relevant actors on their challenges.

Easy to understand mini-guides

The team establishes a knowledge base that is updated as the program develops. Two mini-guides have been published in PDF format, one on the success of an EIGchallenge and the other on opening up the source code of the software produced.

A dedicated team

Within Etalab, a dedicated team sets up the promotion and supports it for 10 months. This multidisciplinary team can back EIGs and mentors in various aspects of their challenges: strategy, technical, project management, assessment, communication, etc.

The Ecosystem

The EIG program is the community's gamble: in working closely with an entire ecosystem, it can offer comprehensive backing to EIGs and their mentors.

Etalab pprovides both legal and technical support and gives the EIG-mentor tandems the benefit of its knowledge of administrative networks. For example, EIGs benefit from preferential interactions with the members of the data.gouv.fr team who coach them on technical issues.

Beta.gouv is a mirror device of the EIG program which allows EIG-mentors tandems to benefit from its experience in backing digital projects in administrations. The synergies are so strong that several EIG challenges are part of state-owned start-ups: Tell-tell signs, Explocode and LexImpact.

EIGs strengthen their group working in contact with other public interest digital players: public players such as the CNIL, working groups and associations such as DataForGood or Latitudes, etc.
